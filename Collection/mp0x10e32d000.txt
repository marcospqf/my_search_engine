<p style="text-align: left;"><strong>Welcome to the Digital 100: The World's Most Valuable Internet Startups!</strong></p>
<p style="text-align: left;">In this year's list, we've looked at and evaluated more than 300 startups and ranked the top 100.</p>
<p style="text-align: left;">We're looking at common stock value here--the price the public market might put on the company (not the valuations achieved using sweetheart terms in some preferred stock deals).</p>
<p style="text-align: left;">So what are the 100 most valuable digital startups in the world?</p>
<p style="text-align: left;"><a href="http://www.businessinsider.com/digital-100/1-facebook-1"><strong>Facebook</strong></a> tops our list again, with a valuation of $25 billion, up radically from $6.5 billion last year.  Facebook continues to grow at a tremendous clip and now has more than 500 million users worldwide. The company will generate revenue of over $1 billion in 2010 and become cash flow positive. </p>
<p style="text-align: left;">Rounding out the top five: <a href="http://www.businessinsider.com/digital-100/2-zynga-2"><strong>Zynga</strong></a>, the social game developer; <a href="http://www.businessinsider.com/digital-100/3-wikimedia-foundation-wikipedia-3"><strong>Wikipedia</strong></a>, the free encyclopedia we think could be worth $5 billion if it tried to make money; <a href="http://www.businessinsider.com/digital-100/4-skype-4"><strong>Skype</strong></a>, which might go for $5 billion in an open market; and <a href="http://www.businessinsider.com/digital-100/5-craigslist-5"><strong>Craigslist</strong></a>, the wildly popular listings site that could coin money if it ever decided to do so.</p>
<h2 style="text-align: left;"><a href="http://www.businessinsider.com/digital-100/1-facebook-1"><strong><strong>Click here to scroll through the Digital 100 </strong></strong></a></h2>
<div style="padding: 0pt 0pt 20px 20px; width: 410px; float: right;">
<p><strong>About The List</strong></p>
<p>Last year, we expanded our original list, the SAI 25, into the <a href="http://www.businessinsider.com/sai-50-2009">SAI 50+</a>. We valued and ranked some of the worlds leading private online companies.</p>
<p>This year we expanded our search and analysis yet again. We found a ton more companies that are earning a lot of money and / or growing their businesses rapidly. The result is this years new and improved "Digital 100" -- 100 of the world's most valuable private digital startups.</p>
<p>Notable companies not included on<span class="KonaFilter image-container float_right" style="width:212px"><span data-post-image class="image"><a href="http://www.businessinsider.com/digital-100/1-facebook-1"><img src="http://static2.businessinsider.com/image/4c9a491e7f8b9aeb66470700/digital-100-logo-circle.png" border="0" alt="Digital 100 logo circle" width="212" height="212"></a><span class="source-only"></span></span><p></span> last years list include <a href="http://www.businessinsider.com/digital-100/11-groupon-11"><strong>Groupon</strong></a>, the beloved daily deal site.</p>
<p><strong>What's New</strong></p>
<p>A lot has happened in the past year: The economy crept back from the beating it took the last two years, venture funding trickled in for category winners, and online advertising staged a comeback (of sorts).  E-commerce and virtual goods sales, meanwhile, continued to hold up relatively well.</p>
<p>As a result, there are some changes to last years rankings. Most notably, <a href="http://www.businessinsider.com/digital-100/10-linkedin-10"><strong>LinkedIn</strong></a>, the professional networking site, doubled in value, perhaps reflecting the shift in job-hunting strategies with the rebounding economy.</p>
<p><strong>Methodology</strong></p>
<p>We used the same valuation methodology as have the last two years, which you can read about in detail <a href="http://www.businessinsider.com/sai-25-valuation-methodology">here</a>. Obviously, our valuations are only as good as the information we have, so please feel free to comment in the post or send an email to <em>afusfeld@businessinsider.com</em>.</p>
</div>
